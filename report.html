The composite score

Every pillar produces a 0-100 score. The composite is a weighted sum: each pillar's score × its weight. Weights add up to 1.0. The result is rounded and mapped to a letter grade: 90+ A+, 80-89 A, 70-79 B+, 60-69 B, 50-59 C+, 40-49 C, 30-39 D, under 30 F.

Pillar weights

  • Content Extractability — 20%
  • Schema Coverage — 15%
  • AI Crawler Access — 12%
  • Entity & Brand Authority — 12%
  • Live Citation Test — 12%
  • E-E-A-T Signals — 10%
  • FAQ Coverage — 8%
  • Freshness — 6%
  • llms.txt Presence — 5%

Severity levels

  • CRITICAL: Blocking — AI engines can't see you at all (robots.txt block, no JSON-LD anywhere, total citation absence)
  • HIGH: Major pillar lift available, single fix unlocks 30+ pillar points
  • MEDIUM: Real issue, 10-20 pillar point lift, usually a per-page fix
  • LOW: Polish — cosmetic or bonus signal

Score lift caveat

The "+N" on each finding is the lift to its pillar score, not to the composite. To estimate composite impact, multiply by the pillar weight. Example: a HIGH finding worth +50 to a 12% pillar = ~6 composite points.
