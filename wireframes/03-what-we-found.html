Intent: Kevin's micro-commitment mechanic. User reads each card, clicks "Fix this" or "Skip", their selections accumulate into the tally bar at top. By Step 4 they've actively built their own priorities — high conversion to the "yes" close.

States visible above: 2 cards "Saved" (committed style, darker border + filled button), 3 cards "Fix this" (default state, secondary button). Real interaction: clicking "+ Fix this" turns the card into the committed state + bumps the tally.

Severity vocabulary (3 levels, not 4): CRITICAL (blocking — invisible to AI), WARNING (real gap, fixable), OPPORTUNITY (upside framing, not a problem). Replaces v2's "+50 pillar lift" jargon.

Persistence: selections saved to URL fragment or localStorage so refresh + back-button work. Posted to backend at Step 4 entry so sales has the priority list before the call.
